About Me

I am a full-stack Javascript boot-camp student with current experience using HTML/CSS/jQuery/React to build web apps on the front end and Node.js, Express and PostgreSQL on the back. With a background in events management, the smallest details matter, and that is something equally as important in programming. I'm not afraid to ask why, and I thrive when I can understand the end goal while diving into the details. Constantly looking to learn and expand my knowledge base is something I strive for each and every day.


After spending close to 10 years in the Events Marketing industry, I was, along with many others, left unemployed due to the effects of Covid-19 on the economy. I used this time to reflect and recalibrate on what I wanted to do with my career going forward. After making the decision to enroll in Thinkful's Software Engineering Program, the prospect of being able to enter this field and work along some expert programmers is now a reality. Offering a full front to back end experience building out websites and apps to clients is something I look forward to being able to offer.

When I am not coding, exercising takes up my free time. In addition to chasing around a toddler, I am an avid runner and have completed to date 3 marathons and over 10 halfs. I also love to travel (you know, when that becomes a thing again). The excitement of traveling to new places and experiencing new cultures is something that never gets old. Always having the desire to learn, both in life and professionally, is a driving force in my life. I am also a huge fan of Disney.

Github Finder

React application to search through GitHub users. The technology stack for this application includes JavaScript, React and CSS. Utilizing the GitHub API, this application lets you search for a name or username of any GitHub user and then presents you with a streamlined user bio page along with their most recent five projects. News of Yesteryear

This was my first capstone project for my Thinkful Bootcamp. It is an app based on my love for microfilm. Utilizing the Library of Congresses Chronicling America API, you can search any topic be presented with digital scans of newspapers pertaining to that term dating from 1963 all the way back to 1789! The technology stack for this app included HTML, CSS, JavaScript, jQuery, and Restful APIs.
